What are the new tags added in HTML5? 

HTML5 introduced several new tags that were not available in previous versions. Some of these new tags are:

<header>: Used to define introductory content for a section or a page.

<footer>: Specifies the footer for a section or a page.

<nav>: Represents a section with navigation links.

<article>: Defines an independent piece of content, such as a blog post or a news article.

<section>: Represents a thematic grouping of content within a document.

<aside>: Indicates content aside from the main content, such as sidebars or related content.

<main>: Specifies the main content of the document, excluding header, footer, and navigation content.

<figure> and <figcaption>: Used together to represent self-contained content, such as images or illustrations, along with a caption.

<time>: Specifies a specific time or date, useful for marking up dates and times in a machine-readable format.

<progress>: Displays the progress of a task, typically with a progress bar.

<meter>: Represents a scalar measurement within a known range, such as disk usage or temperature.
